AuditBoard: A11y + i18n Audit

Accessibility and internationalization scores for the 23 user-facing modules in auditboard-frontend, with per-module risks and recommendations.

March 27, 2026

Strongest patterns found
  • WorkStream and ASQ lead the field across all 23 modules: both score 78+ on a11y and 85+ on i18n, and serve as internal reference implementations.
  • i18n health is consistently stronger than a11y across all 23 modules. The formatMessage + ICU pattern is well established and broadly adopted.
  • Luna components provide a solid a11y baseline. Modules that rely heavily on Luna primitives (Exceptions, ITRM, Integrations) inherit correct ARIA structure with little extra effort.
  • Exceptions and ITRM demonstrate exemplary SchemaForm adoption with consistent modal patterns that other modules should emulate.
Top cross-cutting gaps
  • Missing aria-label on interactive elements recurs across Controls, Issues, OpsAudit, TPRM, and Settings; icon-only buttons, form inputs, and table actions are the most common offenders. @tooltip is not a screen reader substitute.
  • Missing aria-live regions for async state changes (loading states, save confirmations, analysis completion) is absent in most modules; no consistent pattern for announcing dynamic content updates.
  • Dashboard has the lowest scores (a11y 62, i18n 68): Sigma iframe embeds lack accessible titles, and API-sourced enum strings have no i18n mapping layer.
  • Hardcoded English strings persist in TPRM settings (.gjs files) and shared/ components; low formatMessage adoption in shared/ (14/44 files) propagates i18n gaps across consuming modules.
  • Files module has the lowest smart-forms score (40): google-file-upload.gjs uses native browser APIs and Ember classic Input, bypassing all Luna Form and validation patterns.
